NOTHING WORKS

Berlin underground band since 2022.

BIO

Formed in late 2022, Nothing Works is a rising force from Berlin’s underground subcultures. Blending '90s indie/alternative with modern melodic and post-hardcore, their sound—raw, urgent, and emotionally charged—can be summed up as “heavy melodic hardcore.”

Think Drug Church meets Bikini Kill — that’s a good starting point for understanding their unapologetically political lyrics and the fierce energy they bring to every show.

Their debut EP, Bold Talk for a Burner Account, is set for release in June 2025, featuring four songs that give a clear sense of what they’re about. Brace yourself.

The band, composed entirely of FLINTA* members, is quickly becoming a standout in the local scene, known for both their message and their momentum. Despite being young, Nothing Works has already supported notable acts from Austria, Germany, Mexico, the USA, and Canada — including CURB, PUNITIVE DAMAGE, YEAHRS, SHORELINE, GIRLS GO SKA, GO! NYHC, and DRY SOCKET.

In July 2025, they’ll join Tomar Control from Peru on tour, playing 11 shows across 5 European countries — 3 of them for the first time.

Their mission? To make people move — dance, stage dive, scream — while also raising awareness about political struggles and everyday resistance.

ARTISTIC RESUME

Nothing Works has been actively performing across Berlin and beyond. They’ve played numerous local venues and festivals and have already toured through Austria, the Czech Republic, and cities like Leipzig, Halle, and Dresden. Their high-energy live shows and honest presence on stage have earned them growing recognition in Berlin's independent scene.

In 2024, they released a two songs demo. However, due to recording issues — including technical problems with drum mic placement — the band decided to re-record and expand the project. Their debut EP, now featuring four tracks, is scheduled for release in late June 2025.

In July, they will join Tomar Control (Peru) on a European tour through Germany, Austria, Croatia, Portugal, and Spain. The tour is a key opportunity to present their new EP to international audiences, build professional networks, and grow as a support act within a broader DIY community.
